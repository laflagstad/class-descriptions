## Overview

**Unit Name:** Arctic Barren and Sparsely Vegetated Active Floodplain

**Level:** Map Class

**Code:** 315

**Concept:** This [level] encompasses barren to sparsely vegetated substrates associated with riverine floodplains that experience regular and high-energy flooding. When present, vegetation is comprised of ruderal herbs. Soils are coarse-grained, mineral, and well-drained; permafrost is deep.

## Dynamics

**Dynamics Description:** Across floodplains in the Arctic, both the energy of flooding and depth of seasonal thaw decreases away from the main channel. These gradients are paralleled by decreases in sediment grain size, drainage, and shrub stature and increase in understory development. The erosion and redeposition of sediment associated with frequent and high-energy flooding in the active floodplain limits the development of soils and the establishment and growth of vegetation. Ice blocks may scour substrates during spring breakup. As this type is maintained by hydrology, any moderation of flooding regime, for example through channel migration away from the site, will promote the growth of existing species and the establishment of bryophytes and shrubs.

## Range

**Range Description:** Occurring throughout the Arctic bioclimatic zone, especially across the low-relief terrain of the Arctic Coastal Plain.

**Rarity on Landscape:** Uncommon

## Environment

**Environment Description**: This [level] is associated with the active floodplains of meandering and braided rivers.  Soils are comprised of sand, gravel, and cobble, are well-drained, and free of surface organics. Owing to the high capacity of water to retain heat, permafrost tends to be deep within the active floodplain and is not a formative element.
