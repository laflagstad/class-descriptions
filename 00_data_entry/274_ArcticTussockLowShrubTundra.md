## Vegetation

### Structure

**Structural Description:** This [level] is dominated by an assemblage of tussock-forming sedges and low shrubs. Bryophytes and lichens are common and abundant. Tussocks are the primary structural feature of this type.

### Floristics

**Floristics Description:** This [level] is indicated by low shrubs and *Eriophorum vaginatum* tussocks. Highly constant low shrubs include *Betula nana, Vaccinium uliginosum*, *Salix pulchra*, *Salix richardsonii*, *Rhododendron tomentosum* ssp. *decumbens* and *Alnus viridis* ssp. *fruticosa*. Constant dwarf shrubs include *Vaccinium vitis-idaea*, *Cassiope tetragona*, *Empetrum nigrum*, *Salix reticulata* and *Dryas integrifolia*. Herbs, such as *Carex bigelowii*, *Rubus chamaemorus*, *Polygonum bistorta* ssp. *plumosa*, *Rubus chamaemorus*, *Arctagrostis latifolia*, and *Saussurea angustifolia,* are also highly constant and occur in low abundance. The most common non-vascular species are the mosses *Hylocomium splendens*, *Aulacomnium turgidum*, *Dicranum elongatum*, *Ptilidium ciliare,* *Sphagnum warnstorfii*, and *Tomenthypnum nitens;* and the lichens, *Flavocetraria cucullata, Dactylina arctica, Thamnolia vermicularis, Peltigera apthosa*, *Cladina arbuscula-mitis*, *Sphaerophorus globosus*, and *Cetraria islandica.*

## Dynamics

**Dynamics Description:** Ice-rich permafrost is always present at these sites and thermokarst, cryoturbation, and solifluction are the most common site-level disturbances. At the landscape scale, this type forms stable communities over broad areas.

## Range

**Range Description:** Occuring across the Arcitc bioclimatic zone, especially prevalent in the Brooks Range Foothills.

**Rarity on Landscape:** Ubiquitous

## Environment

**Environment Description**: This [level] occurs in upland and lowland terrain and is commonly associated with polygonal complexes but also occurs on non-patterned ground. Shallow permafrost retards the infiltration of surface water and promotes saturation at depth. A tussock growth form allows graminoids to elevate their live root mass above the level of continual saturation. Soils tend to be circumneutral, cold and poorly drained, silty mineral soils overlain by a thick organic horizon. The occurrence of low shrub tussock tundra tends to increase with increasing slope, whereas dwarf shrub tussock tundra is more associated with wetter, flatter areas on the Arctic Coastal Plain.
