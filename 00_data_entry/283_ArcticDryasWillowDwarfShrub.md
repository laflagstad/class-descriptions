### Floristics

**Floristics Description:** This [level] is indicated by *Dryas ajanensis*, *Dryas alaskensis*, *Dryas hookeriana*, and/or *Dryas integrifolia*. *Dryas ajanensis* is more commonly found on coarse substrates or mineral soil or on acidic substrates with *Salix phlebophylla* or *Luzula* species. *Dryas integrifolia* tends to occur on circumneutral to alkaline substrates with *Salix arctica*, *Salix reticulata*, or *Carex scirpoidea*. Both species often co-occur with the prostrate willows, *Salix polaris* and *Salix rotundifolia*. Accessory forbs may include *Silene acaulis*, *Astragalus umbellatus*, *Tofieldia pusilla*, and *Lupinus arcticus*. Common lichens are *Flavocetraria cucullata*, *Flavocetraria nivalis*, *Thamnolia vermicularis*, *Dactylina arctica*, *Masonhalea richardsonii*, and *Ochrolechia frigida*. *Racomitrium lanuginosum* and *Polytrichum juniperinum* are common mosses.

## Environment

**Environment Description**: Dryas Dwarf Shrub occurs on exposed, windswept areas with little winter snow cover that are underlain by continuous permafrost. This type occupies upland and lowland physiography located between the coastal zone and latitudinal tree line. *Dryas ajanensis*-dominated types are commonly associated with uplands and ridges, as well as on acidic to neutral, organic substrates in lowlands. Sites dominated by *Dryas integrifolia* are more common at higher latitudes and neutral to alkaline, mineral substrates. Where species co-occur, *Dryas ajanensis* occupies drier, more exposed microsites and *Dryas integrifolia* occupies wetter, more protected microsites, such as swales or between hummocks.
