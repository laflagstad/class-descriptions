### Floristics

**Floristics Description:** This [level] is indicated by wetland sedges, such as *Carex aquatilis* and *Eriophorum* species, and hydrophytic mosses (i.e., *Sphagnum* species, *Drepanocladus* species, *Scorpdium scorpoides*, and *Calliergon* species), often occupying moist to wet troughs of high-centered polygons and the surfaces of low-centered polygons. *Salix pulchra* and dwarf ericaceous shrubs, such as *Rhododendron tomentosum* and *Vaccinium vitis-idaea*, often occupy moist microhighs in complex with these peatlands.

## Environment

**Environment Description:** This [level] frequently occurs in drained lake beds and low-lying depressions with poor drainage. Polygonal complexes form over ice-rich permafrost that undergoes seasonal freeze-thaw cycles, causing the ground to contract and expand. As the ground contracts, cracks form and fill with water, which subsequently freezes, creating ice wedges beneath polygon margins. Over time, continued ice wedge growth can heave overlying soil upward, forming raised rims. The centers remain low and may fill with water, creating low-centered polygons. Under the right conditions, peat will rapidly accumulate in the moist depressions of polygons and thicken, filling polygon ponds and adjacent troughs (Britton 1967).
