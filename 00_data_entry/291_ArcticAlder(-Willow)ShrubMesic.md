## Overview

**Unit Name:** Arctic Alder (-Willow) Shrub Mesic

**Level:** Map Class

**Code:** 291

**Concept:** This [level] encompasses mesic, open to closed, tall shrublands dominated by *Alnus alnobetula* ssp. *fruticosa,* often in combination with willows, such as *Salix alaxensis*. A relativbely stable type subject to coluvial processes.

## Vegetation

### Structure

**Structural Description:** This [level] is dominated by tall broad-leaved deciduous shrubs. Low shrubs and forbs occur in lower abundance, but do not assume dominance. Bryophytes are common and abundant, whereas lichens may occur less frequently.

**Structural Class:** low shrub, tall shrub

### Floristics

**Floristics Description:** This [level] is indicated by *Alnus alnobetula* ssp. *fruticosa.* Tall and low willow shrubs, such as *Salix alaxensis, Salix pulchra, Salix richardsonii, Salix barclayii,* and *Salix commutata* are common and may co-dominate*.* Other dwarf and prostrate shrubs, including *Vaccinium* species, *Betula nana, Empetrum nigrum,* or *Rhododendron tomentosum,* are common in the understory. Common herbs include *Calamagrostis canadensis, Chamerion angustifolium, Equisetum arvense, Rubus arcticus,* and *Sanguisorba canadensis.* The most common mosses are *Hylocomium splendens* and *Dicranum* species.

## Dynamics

**Dynamics Description:** The [unit_name] type is relatively stable. On steeper slopes, sites may be succeptible to soil creep, landslide, and avalanche. Permafrost is present at depth but is not a formative element. This type is expected to expand its range under a warming climate. 

## Range

**Range Description:** Occurring throughout the Arctic bioclimatic zone, associated with the Brooks Range foothills.

**Rarity on Landscape:** Uncommon

## Environment

**Environment Description**: This [level] forms at variable elevations up to 600 m on concave or convex slopes on moderately steep headwater floodplains, hillside colluvium, glacial deposits, solifluction deposits, talus, and upland loess. The presence of near-surface permafrost inhibits the establishment and growth of tall shrubs in Arctic regions; thus, alder distribution in the Arctic is largely limited to floodplains or small patches where the depth of seasonal thaw is greater.
