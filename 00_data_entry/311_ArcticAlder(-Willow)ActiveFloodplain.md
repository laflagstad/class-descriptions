## Overview

**Unit Name:** Arctic Alder (-Willow) Active Floodplain

**Level:** Map Class

**Code:** *311*

**Concept:** This [level] encompasses open or closed tall shrublands dominated by *Alnus alnobetula* ssp. *fruticosa* and species of *Salix* occurring on the portions of riverine floodplains that experience irregular flooding or saturation. Soils are fine-grained sands and silts interbedded with organic matter.  Permafrost is present at depth, but is not a formative element.

### Floristics

**Floristics Description:** This [level] is dominated or co-dominated by the alder *Alnus alnobetula* ssp. *fruticosa* and a combination of willow species including *Salix alaxensis*, *Salix richardsonii*, *Salix niphoclada*, and *Salix glauca*. The presence of *Salix alaxensis* increases towards the active floodplain. Diagnostic herbaceous plants include the forbs *Eurybia sibirica*, *Artemisia tilesii*, *Parnassia kotzebuei*, *Tanacetum bipinnatum* ssp. *bipinnatum*, *Chamerion latifolium*,*Astragalus alpinus, Astragalus umbellatus, Polemonium acutiflorum*, the horsetails *Equisetum arvense* and *E. variegatum*, and the grass *Festuca rubra*. The presence of dwarf shrubs and mosses increases in settings that are either rarely flooded or seasonally saturated. Here, common shrubs are *Arctous rubra, Dryas integrifolia*, and *Salix reticulata* and bryophytes include *Tomentypnum nitens, Hylocomium splendens, Aulacomnium palustre, Aulacomnium turgidum*, and *Campylium stellatum*.

## Dynamics

**Dynamics Description:** The [unit_name] experiences irregular flooding or saturation during the growing season. Across floodplains in the Arctic, both the energy of flooding and depth of seasonal thaw decreases away from the main channel. These gradients are paralleled by decreases in sediment grain size, drainage, and shrub stature and increase in understory development. Owing to the comparatively lower tolerance to inundation and sedimentation of alder relative to willow, this type is restricted to the inactive floodplain and protected areas of the active floodplain. Here, lower energy and less frequent flooding is often accompanied by the deposition of fine-grained sediment. As the rate of organic matter deposition exceeds sedimentation shrublands on the inactive floodplain tend to be shorter with a more open canopy structure and greater cover of bryophytes due to less frequent erosion. As this type is maintained by hydrology, any change to the frequency of flooding and duration of inundation or saturation will have commensurate effects on vegetation. For example, as the main channel migrates across the valley floor, sites can become removed from the active or inactive floodplain, eventually transitioning to upland tundra.

## Environment

**Environment Description**: This [level] forms linear bands across low gradient riverine floodplains that are subject to irregular flooding or saturation. Soils are seasonally wet following overbank flooding in the spring but are otherwise mesic to dry.  As the energy and frequency of flooding decrease with distance from the main channel, soils in the protected active and inactive floodplain tend to be fine-grained sands and silts interbedded with organic matter. Deposition of nutrient-rich sediment promotes the growth of a species-rich and semi-continuous understory. Permafrost is present at depth, but is not a formative element.
